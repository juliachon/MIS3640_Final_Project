PROJECT OBJECTIVE:


We initially decided to analyze Airbnb listings in New York City for our project. Our goal was to help Airbnb hosts find a suitable price based on the different variables of our dataset. After meeting with Professor Li, we realized that we needed to concentrate on a concrete idea, and that we should better integrate what we learned in class, because our project was too focused on data science.
We also realized that we would have had difficulties accomplishing our goals for our project if we continued with New York City. For example, we wanted to highlight each neighborhood’s key landmarks and data values. It would have been difficult with New York City because New York City has 5 boroughs that are subdivided into over 250 unique neighborhoods. Since we had to research landmarks and add the markers individually (even with a for loop), it would have taken precious time we could use to focus on making our project and code more robust.
We decided to use Boston’s dataset instead. Since we had already coded the exploratory data analysis functions for New York City, it was a very easy switch. With the listings in Boston’s dataset, we changed our goal to help Airbnb users (which includes Airbnb hosts and guests) rather than just the host. For example, if the host was to look for a cheaper neighborhood to rent a property or neighborhoods with fewer listings and competition, they could look at the average prices per neighborhood through the interactive map to decide what price to charge.
